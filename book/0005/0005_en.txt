The current state of knowledge and the currently discussed biochemical and psychological models do not allow a holistic understanding of the functioning of the human brain. This is not surprising when one takes into account that emotions, for example, are cause as well as effect and constantly influence one another. It remains fascinating to take a step closer to understanding the brain and consciousness, and there is much to be done in the future too. Regardless of the power of innovation and creativity shown in this book, the small phenethylamine scaffold still allows numerous new, undiscovered modifications. The fascination for the PEA remains unbroken; the question of “why” is a question for each of us. This book tells of what has already been accomplished in this area.
